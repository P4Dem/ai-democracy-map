## Detailed Code Descriptions

### 1. Citizenship, Law and Rights

Concerns the rights of citizens and the state's ability to guarantee equal rights through constitutional and legal processes — including civil, political, economic and social rights.

#### 1.1 Nationhood and Citizenship

Effects on the inclusivity of citizenship and the equal recognition and protection of minorities, vulnerable groups, and those at the margins of political life — including whether all people are equally seen, served, and protected by the state, or whether existing patterns of exclusion and discrimination are entrenched or deepened.

Coded items affect who is recognised and treated as a full citizen or rights-holder, creates differential visibility or protection across groups, or produces or reinforces arbitrary discrimination against minorities or marginalised populations.

#### 1.2 Rule of Law and Access to Justice

Effects on the integrity of legal processes, judicial independence, and citizens' ability to obtain fair treatment and redress; on whether public officials and institutions are subject to transparent and consistently applied rules; and on whether accountability for harms is clear and enforceable.

Coded items affect legal processes or judicial independence, citizens' ability to obtain fair treatment or redress, or when accountability for harms caused by AI systems is unclear or absent.

#### 1.3 Civil and Political Rights

Effects on the freedoms of expression, movement, association, and assembly; on freedom from physical violation or fear; on freedom from surveillance, profiling, and censorship; and on the protection of those who defend human rights and democratic values.

Coded items involve surveillance, profiling, censorship, or intimidation of citizens or civil society; restricts freedom of expression or assembly; or silences individuals on the basis of their beliefs or political activity.

#### 1.4 Economic and Social Rights

Effects on access to work, social security, and basic necessities; on health and education; and on the broad and equitable distribution of economic gains across society.

Coded items affect material conditions, labour rights, access to essential services, or the distribution of economic power and wealth — including where AI concentrates economic gains in the hands of a few or displaces workers from their livelihoods.

### 2. Representative and Accountable Government

Concerns the institutions and processes of representative and accountable government: electoral processes and political parties; the effectiveness, transparency, and integrity of public administration, legislature and judiciary; civilian control of security forces; and the absence of illegitimate influence on policy-making.

#### 2.1 Free and Fair Elections

Effects on the integrity of electoral processes, the inclusivity and accessibility of voting, the fairness of political competition, and public trust in elections as genuinely open contests.

Coded items affect electoral integrity, voter access, campaign fairness, or public confidence in election outcomes.

#### 2.2 The Democratic Role of Political Parties

Effects on the freedom of parties to form, recruit and campaign; and on whether political competition between parties remains fair and free from undue external influence or structural advantage.

Coded items affect how parties organise, compete or finance themselves, or where AI gives some parties a structural advantage over others.

#### 2.3 Effective and Accountable Government

Effects on the capacity of government to deliver public services fairly and implement policy faithfully; on the competence, transparency, and integrity of public administration; and on whether the systems and technologies governments use to exercise state power remain open to democratic scrutiny — including where dependencies on private or foreign providers shift effective control over state functions beyond democratic reach.

Coded items affect how well government serves citizens equitably, how faithfully it carries out its democratic mandate, how honestly and transparently it administers public affairs, or whether the operational tools of government — AI systems, data infrastructure, decision-support platforms — remain answerable to democratic institutions rather than to private interests.

#### 2.4 The Democratic Effectiveness of the Legislature

Effects on the capacity of parliament and other democratic institutions to scrutinise legislation and hold the executive to account; on the independence of the legislature from the executive; and on their capacity to receive and process genuine input.

Coded items affect legislative scrutiny, parliamentary oversight capacity, the balance of power between executive and legislature, or the ability of democratic institutions to remain genuinely accessible and responsive to citizen input.

#### 2.5 Democratic Role of the Judiciary

Effects on the capacity of courts to provide independent oversight of the executive and legislature; on judicial review of laws and government decisions; and on the ability of citizens to challenge the exercise of public power through legal means.

Coded items affect judicial independence, constitutional review of AI-related legislation or government decisions, or citizens' ability to seek legal redress against automated or AI-assisted exercises of state power.

#### 2.6 National–Subnational Power Relations

Effects on the independence and capacity of sub-central tiers of government; on local accountability and responsiveness to citizens; and on the distribution of power and resources between central and local government.

Coded items explicitly affect the balance of power between central and local government — including where AI-driven centralisation of data or infrastructure shifts effective power upward — or where it affects the ability of local or regional government to act independently and remain accountable to local communities. Where the concern is primarily about government capacity or service delivery rather than the central-local power balance, prefer 2.3.

#### 2.7 Civilian Control of the Military and Police

Effective civilian control over armed forces and security services; public accountability of police; absence of unaccountable private security actors.

Coded items affect what security or enforcement agencies can do, what civilians and oversight bodies can see or check about them, or who commands them — i.e. when the vertical accountability relationship between civilians/democratic institutions and security services is affected.

#### 2.8 Illegitimate Influence over Policy-Making

Effects on the extent to which powerful actors shape policy through back channels — such as hidden lobbying, capturing regulators, or flooding consultations with artificial responses — rather than through open and accountable processes, and on how far policy outputs and outcomes reflect what the public actually wants.

Coded items show AI enabling or amplifying corruption, conflicts of interest, or non-transparent private influence over public policy; when it allows well-resourced actors to shape regulation, legislation, or public consultations through back channels, manufactured consensus, or lobbying at scale; or when it gives private interests disproportionate influence over individual officials or the policy process more broadly.

### 3. Civil Society and Popular Participation

Concerns the conditions for an alert and active citizen body: the independence and viability of professional journalism; and the formation, exchange, and contestation of political opinion.

#### 3.1 Independent and Fact-Based Journalistic Media

Effects on the independence and pluralism of regulated, professional journalism; on the representativeness of opinion and the investigative capacity of journalism; on journalists' ability to operate freely; and on the accountability of platforms for the information flows they shape and amplify.

Coded items affect the conditions under which regulated, professionally produced journalism operates — including its independence, revenue, ownership, investigative capacity, or distribution reach — or when it affects the accountability of platforms specifically in their role as distributors of journalistic content.

#### 3.2 Opinion Formation and Political Participation

Effects on the conditions under which citizens form, exchange, and revise political views — including through social media and other non-journalistic channels; on the depth, breadth, and authenticity of citizen participation in public life; on equal access to public voice across social groups; and on the range and independence of civil society and social movements.

Coded items affect how citizens form or express political views, organise collectively, or participate in public life; when AI shapes the information environment through which public opinion is formed, including social media, persuasion, disinformation, and echo chambers; or when it affects the capacity of civil society to act independently and authentically.

### 4. Transnational Dynamics

Concerns the international dimensions of democracy: external influences on a country's democracy, and the country's democratic impact abroad.

#### 4.1 External Influences on the Country's Democracy

Effects on a country's freedom from external interference in its democratic processes; on the geopolitical balance between democratic and autocratic models of governance; and on the ability of democratic states to maintain autonomous control over their political institutions and infrastructure.

Coded items involve AI-enabled foreign interference in domestic democratic processes, or where the competitive advantage of AI-enabled autocracies — in governance capacity, surveillance, or information operations — affects the broader democratic-autocratic balance.

#### 4.2 The Country's Democratic Impact Abroad

Effects on a country's support for human rights and democracy in other countries; on respect for international rule of law; and on whether a country's policies, exports, and international conduct strengthen or undermine democratic governance elsewhere.

Coded items affect how the coded country shapes democratic conditions outside its borders — through export of AI tools, participation in global AI governance, or setting regulatory precedents other states follow.

## How to Code

The following rules apply to every coding decision and are intended to keep coding consistent across items and coders.

- **Code all applicable categories.** Include both direct and secondary or trickle-down effects. There is no limit on the number of codes per item, but each must be justifiable.

- **Ground code in the source where possible.** If the democratic impact in a given category cannot be derived from the original text, flag as P4Dem inference.

- **The most direct effect is primary; weaker effects are secondary.** For threats and opportunities, coders may mark one code as primary and others as secondary where it is useful to distinguish, but this is optional — the priority is completeness. For solutions, the primary code is inherited from the connected threat; secondary codes may be added where the solution has broader democratic reach beyond that threat's primary impact area.

- **Government vs. civil society.** Pillar 2 covers what governments do; Pillar 3 covers what citizens and civil society can do. When an item touches both, code in both pillars.

- **Cross-cutting items** (e.g. AI literacy, interdisciplinary research, public interest technologists) are coded to every category their mechanism of democratic effect touches.
